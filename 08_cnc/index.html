Week 8: CNC Milling

Favorite week so far! The CNC setup was a handful (huge thanks to Kassia for the patience), but seeing a flat board turn into a guitar body never gets old. I’m already plotting what to mill next… time permitting.

At a glance
Machine:ShopBot
CAD/CAM:Fusion 360 → Rhino (prep)
Material:Wood stock (measured with calipers)
Workflow:Design → CAM → Zero → Cut
Team:Worked with Cinar
TA assist:Kassia (setup & sanity checks)

1) CNC Milling with the ShopBot

I modeled an electric guitar in Fusion 360. I used a reference outline for the body curve, then designed the internal cavities myself. Early on, I learned the hard way that mixing features across multiple sketches can bite you later when generating toolpaths—keep related geometry together and name your sketches.

With Cinar, we measured the board with calipers, imported the sketches into Rhino, and organized the cut groups. The goal was a layered interior: the corner lines step down at slightly different depths to echo the star motif in the center. That meant careful layer separation—at one point, I had merged too much into a single layer and had to rebuild the CAM groups. Worth it for the final look.

Long story short: lots of passes, lots of brush-vacuuming, and a really satisfying reveal. The stepped pockets around the star came out mesmerizing.

2) Vacuum Forming

We used the guitar body for a vacuum-formed shell. Huge thanks to Jessica for the walkthrough. Pro tip we learned the slow way: the plastic sheet has two protective films. Peel both, or you’ll end up with a mystery hole or a wrinkly pull.

First pull had a small tear (hello, forgotten film), but attempt two was clean. Even with the hiccup, the formed shell captured the edges better than we expected.

3) Molding & Casting

For casting, we picked an air-dry mix that paired well with the plastic mold. Following the instructions, we mixed powder into water at a 5:1 ratio until it hit a thick oobleck vibe, poured, and let it cure for two days.

The surface isn’t perfect, but it’s honestly better than I expected for a first pour. Next time I’ll add a tiny draft on interior edges and tap the mold longer to chase bubbles.

Lessons Learned

  • Plan for layers in CAD. If you want stepped depths, keep sketches/layers clean and named from the start.
  • Measure twice, zero once. Calipers + a consistent Z zero saved us re-runs.
  • Plastic has two films. Yes, both sides. Ask me how I know.
  • Casting loves patience. Mix slowly, pour steadily, tap out bubbles, and walk away.

Next time I use the ShopBot, I’ll be extra mindful of how linework groups into toolpaths and where transitions happen. Cleaner CAM upfront = faster, prettier cuts.
