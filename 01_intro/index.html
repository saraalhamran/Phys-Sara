Week 1: Final Project Proposal

Here are my top ideas in order:

Idea 1: Study with Teddy

To build my Teddy Focus Buddy, I’ll first design a bear-shaped box with ears, a main body housing, a phone holder platform, and a sliding shutter, adding precise cutouts for LEDs, buttons, the OLED screen, and USB ports. I’ll 3D print the teddy face, body, and phone holder, while laser-cutting the shutter from acrylic to ensure a smooth, clean edge that slides easily. Inside, I’ll wire an Arduino Nano as the controller, connecting it to a servo motor to move the acrylic shutter electronically, RGB LEDs for colorful light effects, an OLED display to show my study timer, push buttons for starting and stopping the timer, and optionally a buzzer for audio alerts. I’ll use a breadboard and jumper wires for testing all connections first, then solder everything neatly once it’s working. I’ll power the circuit either through USB or a small LiPo battery, depending on whether I want it portable. After assembling all printed and cut parts with screws or super glue, I’ll upload my custom Arduino code using the Arduino IDE on my laptop, ensuring the shutter opens and closes for study and break modes, the LEDs change color to indicate different focus states, and the display shows countdown times.

Idea 2: Posture Pal

Problem: Poor posture is a common issue caused by long periods of sitting and device use, often leading to back pain, fatigue, and long-term health problems. Most people are unaware when they begin to slouch, making it hard to build consistent posture habits without external feedback.

Solution: Posture Pal detects when your posture deviates from an upright position and gently alerts you (via vibration), helping you build awareness and form healthy posture habits over time, without distractions or shaming.

Creation: To create Posture Pal, I will 3D model and print a small clip-on case using Fusion 360, designed to be clipped on your upper back. Inside, I will install an Arduino Nano, MPU6050 sensor, LiPo battery, and vibration motor. The Arduino will be programmed to detect slouching using posture angle data and activate the motor for feedback.

Idea 3: Focus Vault

Problem: Constant phone distractions reduce productivity and focus, especially during study sessions. Even with self-discipline, the temptation to check notifications or scroll endlessly.

Solution: Focus Vault is a physical lockbox that securely holds your phone while you study, only unlocking in the case of an emergency call. This eliminates casual phone use while still preserving essential access.

Creation: To create Focus Vault, I will design and 3D print a phone-sized lockbox with a secure lid mechanism. Inside, an Arduino Nano will be connected to a servo lock, a SIM module (or Bluetooth/phone detection module), and a battery. The system will monitor for emergency signals (like phone call buzzing), unlocking only under those conditions.

Idea 4: Hands-Free Textbook

Problem: Physical textbooks are heavy and hard to manage, requiring frequent page turns that interrupt focus, especially when your hands are busy taking notes or typing.

Solution: The Hands-Free Textbook Assistant automatically turns pages on command, allowing users to study seamlessly without interrupting their workflow. This supports deeper focus, accessibility, and more efficient studying.

Creation: To create the Hands-Free Textbook Assistant, I will design and 3D print a foldable book stand with integrated mechanical arms for page flipping. Using an Arduino Nano, servo motors and sensors, the system will detect a signal and flip the page accordingly. I will prototype the mechanism, test different paper thicknesses and bindings to ensure that pages are flipped adequately.
